Dirty7

Winning the Game

The player with the least number of points at the end of the game wins.

Basics

  1. Each card has points determined by the rank (suit doesn't matter)
    Card ...
    Points 0 1 2 3 ... 10 10 10 10
  2. On your turn, you must either play (exchange) cards or declare.
  3. Playing cards
    1. Select one or more cards from your hand that you wish to discard. Selected cards appear raised.
    2. Also select exactly one card from either the face up cards on the board or from the facedown pile.
    3. Press "Play" to complete your turn. The selected cards from your hand are exchanged with the single card selected from the board if it is a valid exchange of cards. A valid exchange of cards is determined by the exchange rules in effect for the round.
  4. Declaring
    1. You must have "Declare under points" (default is 7 points) or fewer in your hand to declare.
    2. The round ends when a player declares.
    3. A declare is a claim that the player alone has the lowest number of points.
  5. Scoring
    1. If the player who declared has the lowest number of points alone, the player is awarded 0 points. All other players are awarded the number of points in their hand.
    2. If the player who declared does not have the lowest number of points alone, the player is awarded a penalty of "Penalty points" (default: 40). The player with the lowest number of points is awarded 0 points. Every other player is awarded the number of points in their hand.
  6. The game is played in rounds. The score is tallied at the end of each round. If a player reaches or exceeds the "Game end points" cut-off (default of 100 points), the game stops and one or more players, with the least number of points, are declared winners.

Card Exchange Rules

Basic

The player may drop one or more cards of the same rank. Examples
  • a single queen
  • two sevens
  • three aces
  • found tens

Seq3

The player may drop a sequence of 3 cards or a single card. The suit need not match. Ace has the rank of 1. Examples
  • ace, duece, trey
  • five, six, seven
  • jack, queen, king
  • single four
Invalid moves
  • ace, king, queen
  • two fives

Basic, Seq3

Both basic and seq3 combinations are allowed.

Seq3+

The player may drop a sequence of 3 or more cards or a single card. The suit need not match. Ace has the rank of 1. Examples
  • ace, duece, trey, four
  • five, six, seven
  • nine, ten, jack, queen, king
  • single four

Basic, Seq3+

Both basic and seq3+ combinations are allowed.
